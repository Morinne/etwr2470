Introducing: The Vagus Nerve

The secret to unlocking your superpower of calm

The vagus nerve impacts many areas of the body.

Scroll down to learn more...

The vagus nerve's
MOST IMPORTANT FUNCTION
is relaying sensory information from body organs to the brain.

It lets your brain know if you're hungry or full.

It lets your brain know if you're SCARED or STRESSED.

The brain SENDS signals along the vagus nerve to release acteylcholine.

The neurotransmitter acetylcholine:

Helps calm you down,

Speeds up digestion, and

Counteracts an overactive immune response

The Face and Throat...

Keep scrolling...

The vagus nerve controls many reflex actions in the face and throat:

Coughing

Sneezing

Swallowing

And vomiting

It is also involved in making sounds.

It sends taste sensations from the foot of the tongue to the brain.

The Heart and Lungs...

Keep scrolling...

The vagus nerve connects to the heart and lungs.

It can slow your heart rate,

Calm your breathing,

And lower your blood pressure

It also let's the brain know if your heart rate and breathing rate speeds up suddenly.

The Stomach and Intestines...

Scroll some more...

The vagus nerve connects to smooth muscle in your stomach and intestines.

It controls digestion by:

increasing bowel movement

and signaling glands to secrete digestive juices

It also let's the brain know if you're hungry or full by:

Signaling if your stomach expands from food

and detecting nutrients that are being absorbed by your gut.
